Four New York State Parks Thrive During Covid

While New Yorkers and tourists alike were kept from doing many different activities during the year 2020 because of Covid, these state parks were visited at a higher rate than the year before.

By Camryn Quick

November 13, 2021

Bayswater Park is amongst the state parks who did not struggle with park visitors during Covid. Credit: NYC Parks.

New York State is known for its beautiful parks that are frequented by visitors and tourists alike. New York has just released the data showing state park visitors for the year 2020, and it appears that the park was a sanctuary for many.

New York City has a few state parks within its region. Analyzing the data from four of the parks, including Bayswater Point State Park, Gantry Plaza Park, Heckscher State Park, and Jones Beach Park, showed that people were not deterred from visiting parks amidst the Covid-19 pandemic.
